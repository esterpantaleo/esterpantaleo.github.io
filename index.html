Ester Pantaleo

I'm a data scientist with a PhD in Physics from the University of Bari, Italy, interested in data visualization. I have done research in different fields, from Physics to Genomics and Econometrics, trying to model and simulate different systems using Bayesian Statistics and Monte Carlo methods. Recently I started developing data visualizations.


In this website I'm collecting some projects I have developed as a researcher or as a freelancer. Currently I'm developing etytree an interactive tool for the visualization of the etymology of words funded by an IEG grant of the Wikimedia Foundation. If you have comments please do not hesitate to contact me at esterpantaleo at gmail dot com.


I am also an aspiring fine artist, painter, sculptor. If you are curious you can check my personal website here. I'm on the job market right now and interested in full time or part time remote work.

tools

  1. etytree

    Website · Github · Bitbucket · grant · demo

    An interactive tool to visualize etymologies and to find etymologically connected words.
    Developed with a Wikimedia Foundation individual engagement grant using data extracted from the English Wiktionary into an RDF database. A project inspired by a personal interest in languages (including programming languages) and etymology, and an interest in open source collaborative projects as well as interactive visualizations.

visualizations

  1. ANPR dashboard

    Website

    A real time interactive dashboard showing progress in the construction of a National Resident Population Register. Developed for the Digital Transformation Team of the Italian Government.

  2. Star cluster evolution

    Website · Github

    An animated and interactive version of the HR diagram that evolves in time from birth to an age of 5 billion years using simulated data of a cluster of 100-star similar to our Sun's birthplace and images from the World Wide Telescope. Made for the Adler Planetarium, Chicago.

  3. Flusso regionale studenti di Ateneo

    Website · Github

    An interactive visualization of the inbound, outbound, and stationary flow of Italian students per region, area of study, and sex using real data from MIUR. Made for the Digital Team of the Italian Government.

  4. Mappa dei quartieri

    Website · Github

    An interactive dashboard to explore and compare different neighborhoods in major Italian cities using data from ISTAT and other public sources. Currently under development for the Digital Team of the Italian Government.

  5. Infografica UNIBO

    Website · Github

    A visualization using open data on schools and courses of the University of Bologna. Currently under development for the Digital Team of the Italian Government.

research

  1. altra

    Paper · Supplementary material · Github

    A Bayesian method for simultaneous transcript reconstruction and abundance estimation with RNA-Seq data from multiple samples. Developed under the supervision of Matthew Stephens, professor at the University of Chicago.

  2. multiseq

    Github

    Collaboration on the development of an R package for multiscale analysis, ongoing work in the Stephens lab at the University of Chicago. The embedded model considers data as an inhomogeneous Poisson process and tests for differences in underlying intensities using a Bayesian multi-scale model.

  3. monomvn

    Paper

    Collaboration on the paper "Shrinkage Regression for Multivariate Inference with Missing Data, and an Application to Portfolio Balancing" published on Bayesian Anal. Vol. 5, N. 2 (2010), 237-262. Contributed to this work with the development of an extension of the model to data with fat tails.
    Joint work with prof. Robert Gramacy at the time lecturer at the University of Cambridge.

  4. Covariance estimation

    Paper · Github

    Paper on the comparison of different portfolio optimization techniques "When do improved covariance matrix estimators enhance portfolio optimization? An empirical comparative study of nine estimators" published on Journal of Quantitative Finance, Vol. 11 (2011) - Issue 7.

  5. Hausdorff clustering

    Paper 1 · Paper 2

    Joint work on the development of a new clustering algorithm based on the Hausdorff distance, also applied to financial time series.
